## Design Context

### Users
The product serves the academic operations of Universitas Merdeka Digital. The interface should primarily support admin staff, while remaining coherent and usable for lecturers, students, and program heads. The core job to be done is managing academic entities and workflows clearly: study programs, students, lecturers, classrooms, schedules, enrollments, KRS, grades, and semester operations.

### Brand Personality
Clear, trustworthy, modern. The voice should feel academically credible and operationally competent, with calm confidence rather than flashy consumer energy. Interfaces should reduce ambiguity, support institutional trust, and still feel polished enough to convey a contemporary digital campus.

### Aesthetic Direction
Favor a clean, minimal, formal-institutional dashboard style. Keep the structure data-first and desktop-friendly, with responsive behavior for smaller screens. Treat light and dark modes as equally supported themes. Use visual restraint, solid hierarchy, and polished surfaces rather than novelty. In the absence of explicit references or color mandates, evolve the current academic/admin aesthetic beyond DaisyUI defaults without becoming playful, trendy, or overly generic SaaS.

### Design Principles
1. Prioritize efficient clarity in every workflow, especially CRUD-heavy admin tasks.
2. Preserve calm institutional trust through disciplined hierarchy, spacing, and predictable interaction patterns.
3. Add modern polish through thoughtful typography, subtle visual refinement, and consistent component behavior.
4. Design every feature for both light and dark themes from the start.
5. Meet WCAG AA expectations and remain comfortable at larger text sizes.
